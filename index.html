Hello!

My name is Bradley Bowles and I'm a bioinformatics developer using multi-omics to improve our understanding of cancer. In my day job I'm a scientist at Tempus AI, where I work on the RNA team to generate information on tumor gene expression and splicing. However, this is my personal site where I also like to exhibit side projects of mine, including rare disease work from my PhD. Recent Work

Official works from Tempus AI that I contributed to recently:
11-2025 | Pan-Cancer Fusion Detection

I supported recent work from Tempus where we evaluated a multi-omics approach to use DNA and RNA for the detection of gene rearrangements in cancer. This retrospective work included 67,278 patients with 43 unique cancer types and indicated that RNA utilization increases the detection rate of gene fusions. The increased RNA detection rate was greatest for BRAF and NRG1 fusions. You can read the full paper here.

09-2025 | xR-IVD 510(k) Clearance

In September 2025, Tempus received 510(k) clearance from the FDA for the xR-IVD assay, which includes detection of gene rearrangements using RNA. I have been very fortunate to work on the development of this device alongside so many talented colleagues, and supported the precision and limit of detection aspects of the submission. You can read the official press release here.

Monospace fonts are dear to many of us. Some find them more readable, consistent, and beautiful, than their proportional alternatives. Maybe we’re just brainwashed from spending years in terminals? Or are we hopelessly nostalgic? I’m not sure. But I like them, and that’s why I started experimenting with all-monospace Web.
